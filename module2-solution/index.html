Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak (Kazakh: бешбармак — "five finger") is the national dish among nomadic Turkic peoples in Central Asia and Russia. It is also known as naryn in Xinjiang, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan.

Baursak

Baursak is a delicious puffy fried bread served on special occasions all over Kazakhstan. This isn’t an everyday bread, but something for an important party, like a wedding, or even a memorial.

Kumis

Is a fermented dairy product traditionally made from mare's milk or donkey milk. The drink remains important to the peoples of the Central Asian steppes, of Huno-Bulgar, Turkic and Mongol origin: Kazakhs, Bashkirs, Kalmyks, Kyrgyz, Mongols, and Yakuts.
